The [Metropolitan Waterfront Alliance](http://www.waterfrontalliance.org/) is hosting its seventh annual [City of Water Day](http://www.cityofwaterday.org/) festival this Saturday, July 12th, at multiple locations in New York Harbor.  Festivities will run from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.  This year [Hoboken is joining in the fun](http://www.nj.com/hudson/index.ssf/2014/07/hoboken_hosts_first_city_of_water_day.html), hosting free kayak and ferry rides, along with a multitude of children’s activities, concerts and boat tours at [Maxwell Place Park](http://www.cityofwaterday.org/activities-maxwell).  A [free ferry](http://www.cityofwaterday.org/locations/) between Maxwell Place Park, Paulus Hook in Jersey City and Governors Island will make five round-trip journeys throughout the day. No tickets are required, but all passengers must disembark at every stop.

On Pier 25 in Manhattan, the [historic buoy tender *Lilac*](http://www.lilacpreservationproject.org/) will be open for tours. [John Skelson’s photo exhibit “Tugboats – Workhorses of NY Harbor”](http://workingharbor.wordpress.com/2014/07/05/tugboats-tugboats-tugboats/) will also be on display on the *Lilac*.   Joining *Lilac* for the celebration at the pier will be the schooner [*A. J. Meerwald*](http://bayshorecenter.org/), and the tug *[Pegasus](http://tugpegasus.org/)* and fireboat *[John J. Harvey.](http://www.fireboat.org/)*  The FDNY fireboat [*Three Forty Three*](http://marine1fdny.com/fireboat_343_new.php) and the schooner *[Sherman Zwicker](http://www.grandbanks.org/sherman-zwicker/)* which will also be open for tours.

On Governor’s Island here will be a [variety of family events](http://cityofwaterday.squarespace.com/children-and-families/) as well as the ever-popular [cardboard kayak race.](http://www.cityofwaterday.org/cardboardkayak)

There will also be boat trips and tours on the schooner *[Adirondack](http://cityofwaterday.squarespace.com/adirondack-boat/),*  the *[Circle Line Brooklyn](http://cityofwaterday.squarespace.com/nyc-audubon-eco-cruise/),* the schooner *[Clipper City](http://cityofwaterday.squarespace.com/clipper-city/?SSScrollPosition=320),* the tug *[Pegasus](http://cityofwaterday.squarespace.com/tug-pegasus/),* the fireboat *[John J. Harvey](http://cityofwaterday.squarespace.com/john-j-harvey/),* and the schooner *[Pioneer](http://cityofwaterday.squarespace.com/pioneertour/).*  [Click here for locations and ticketing information.](http://www.cityofwaterday.org/boat-tours2/)  The boat tours are are completely free of charge. There is, however, a fully refundable reservation fee of $5 to hold your spot.
