![](https://old-salt-blog-media.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/uploads/2011/09/liliac.jpg)If you are in the vicinity tomorrow evening be sure to stop by the historic buoy tender *[LILAC](http://lilacpreservationproject.org/)* at the Hudson River Park’s Pier 25 at North Moore Street, Tribeca, Manhattan, where [Carolina Salguero](http://www.carolinasalguero.com/) & [Jessica DuLong](http://www.jessicadulong.com/) will speak on the [Mariners’ Response to 9/11](http://portsidetanker.blogspot.com/2011/08/portside-newyork-exhibit-about-maritime.html) from  7:00-8:30pm. Carolina Salguero was a  photojournalist on 9/11 and is now Director of [PortSide NewYork](http://portsidetanker.blogspot.com/2011/08/portside-newyork-exhibit-about-maritime.html). Jessica DuLong, is a journalist and author of [My River Chronicles](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B003IWYG66?ie=UTF8&tag=tholsabl-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=390957&creativeASIN=B003IWYG66)  (see our [review here](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2009/11/09/my-river-chronicle-rediscovering-the-america-on-the-hudson-by-jessica-dulong-a-review/).)   She is also Chief Engineer on the historic fireboat *John J. Harvey.* Though retired from service, the *John J. Harvey* was the first fire boat on scene on 9/11, pumping river water around the clock to the firefighters at Ground Zero.  Today, Huffington Post also published “[The Untold Story of Ground Zero Evacuations by Boat](http://www.huffingtonpost.com/jessica-dulong/the-untold-story-of-groun_b_955893.html)” by Jessica DuLong.

The talks are part of a multi-media exhibit (photography, videos and oral history) and presentation about the extraordinary and little-known maritime role in 9/11, from evacuation to rubble removal, produced by [PortSide NewYork,](http://portsidetanker.blogspot.com/2011/08/portside-newyork-exhibit-about-maritime.html) a waterfront-themed non-profit organization.
