A Laysan albatross named [Wisdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wisdom_\(albatross\)), first identified and banded on Midway Atoll in 1956, has hatched a chick at the age of at least 70. Wisdom, who returns each fall toÂ [Midway Atoll National Wildlife Refuge](https://www.fws.gov/refuge/midway_atoll/), outlived most of her mates, and raised over 40 chicks. The typical [albatross life span](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albatross) is generally estimated to be 40-50 years.

The Â United States Geological Survey (USGS) has tracked Wisdom since she was first tagged, and has estimated that Wisdom has flown over 3,000,000 miles (4,800,000 km) since 1956 (approximately 120 times the circumference of the Earth). To accommodate her longevity, the USGS has replaced her tag a total of six times.
