![](https://old-salt-blog-media.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/uploads/2017/08/greatnrtugrace.jpg)For those in and around New York harbor, the [25th Annual Great North River Tugboat Race & Competition](https://gem.godaddy.com/p/e257aa?fe=1&pact=49268-140996971-9861126338-e40f1dd333b3861b0bd608c3ec13a6043c64a911), sponsored by the [Working Harbor Committee](http://workingharbor.org/), is coming up on Sunday, September 3rd. The Parade of Tugs starts on the Hudson River at 10 AM. Free viewing is available at  Pier 84 located at W. 44th Street & Hudson River Park. The tugs will proceed to the starting line at 70th Street. The race begins at 10:30 AM.  Nose-to-nose pushing contests and line-toss competitions will start around 11 AM.  At Noon the amateur line-toss and spinach-eating contests will commence followed by an 1 PM awards ceremony.

For an even better view of the parade of tugs and the tugboat race, there is also a [Circle Line Spectator Boat](https://www.nycharities.org/Events/EventLevels.aspx?etid=10028) available. Boarding begins at 9:00 AM and departure is at 9:30 AM from Pier 83. Tickets are $25 for adults and $12 for kids (3-12 years old.)  [Tickets can be purchased online here](https://www.nycharities.org/Events/EventLevels.aspx?etid=10028).

In addition, there will be a raffle of a framed print, *Schooner Pioneer at Sunset*, by renowned seaport artist [Naima Rauam](http://www.downtownexpress.com/2011/08/24/naima-rauam/). The raffle drawing will take place at the 25th Annual Tugboat Race & Competition on Sunday, Sept. 3 at 2 p.m. on Pier 84 located at West 44th St. & Hudson River Park in Manhattan. You don’t need to be present to win. [Tickets are available here](https://www.nycharities.org/Events/EventLevels.aspx?etid=10069). 

The [*Pioneer,*](https://southstreetseaportmuseum.org/visit/street-of-ships/pioneer/) launched in 1885, hauled sand to iron foundries along the Delaware River and was the first of only two cargo-carrying sloops with wrought iron hulls built in this country. She was donated to the South Street Seaport Museum in 1970.
