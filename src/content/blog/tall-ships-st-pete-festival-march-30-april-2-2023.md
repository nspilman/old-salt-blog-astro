![](https://old-salt-blog-media.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/uploads/2023/03/st-pete-festival.jpg)The city of St. Petersburg, FL is hosting [Tallships® St.Pete](https://tallshipsstpete.com/), a four-day maritime festival from March 30 to April 2, 2023.  The city is partnering with Tall Ships America to co-host a fleet of tall ships along the St Petersburg waterfront.  Tall Ships® St. Pete will be the starting point for the [Tall Ships Challenge®  Gulf Coast Series](https://tallshipsamerica.org/tall-ships-challenge/). The tall ships will race from St.Petersburg to Galveston, TX, and on to Pensacola, FL.

Tall Ships® St. Pete will kick off with a Parade of Sail on Thursday evening. The ships featured in the festival include the [*Elissa*](https://tallshipsamerica.org/vessels/elissa/), an 1877 square-rigged iron barque; the 1894 built schooner [*Ernestina-Morrissey;*](https://tallshipsamerica.org/vessels/ernestina-morrissey/) the *[Nao Trinidad](https://tallshipsamerica.org/vessels/nao-trinidad/),* a Spanish-built replica of the 16th-century flagship of the Magellan-Elcano expedition; and the *[Pride of Baltimore II](https://tallshipsamerica.org/vessels/pride-of-baltimore-ii/),* a topsail schooner built to the lines of an 1812-era Baltimore Clipper.  Also participating are the schooners [*When and If*](https://tallshipsamerica.org/vessels/when-and-if/), [*Glenn L. Swetman,*](https://tallshipsamerica.org/vessels/glenn-l-swetman/) [*Suncoast Horizon*](https://tallshipsamerica.org/vessels/suncoast-horizon/) and the research vessel [*Weatherbird II*](https://tallshipsamerica.org/vessels/r-v-weatherbird-ii/).

The festival will take place in Downtown St. Pete: Port St. Pete – 250 8th Ave SE. St Petersburg, FL 33701 in the Bayboro Harbor adjacent to Albert Whitted Airport and USF St Petersburg Campus[Click here to learn more and to purchase festival and sailaway tickets](https://tallshipsstpete.com/festival-schedule/).
