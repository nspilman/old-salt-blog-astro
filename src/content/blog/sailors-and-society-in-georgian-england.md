[![](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/04/amvbl.jpg)“Sailors and Society in Georgian England : The Home of Lord Nelson, Jane Austen & Jack Aubrey”](http://annemarievictory.com/Lavery%20Brochure%20booklet%202011.pdf) – It sounds a bit like someone’s history thesis but in fact is a ten day luxury tour  organized and escorted by British maritime scholar and [author of more than thirty books](http://www.amazon.com/gp/redirect.html?ie=UTF8&location=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.amazon.com%2Fs%3Fie%3DUTF8%26redirect%3Dtrue%26ref_%3Dsr_tc_2_0%26keywords%3DBrian%2520Lavery%26field-contributor_id%3DB000APH7AQ%26qid%3D1301948122%26sr%3D1-2-ent%26rh%3Di%253Astripbooks%252Ck%253ABrian%2520Lavery&tag=tholsabl-20&linkCode=ur2&camp=1789&creative=390957), [Brian Lavery](http://www.amazon.com/gp/redirect.html?ie=UTF8&location=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.amazon.com%2Fs%3Fie%3DUTF8%26redirect%3Dtrue%26ref_%3Dsr_tc_2_0%26keywords%3DBrian%2520Lavery%26field-contributor_id%3DB000APH7AQ%26qid%3D1301948122%26sr%3D1-2-ent%26rh%3Di%253Astripbooks%252Ck%253ABrian%2520Lavery&tag=tholsabl-20&linkCode=ur2&camp=1789&creative=390957), and  [Annemarie Victory](http://annemarievictory.com/biography.html), the owner of the New York-based specialty tour company that bears her name.  The tour sounds perfect for my wife and me.  I am a fan of Georgian Naval Fiction while my wife is a Janeite, a rabid fan of Jane Austen.  It is, however, a bit beyond my budget at around $10,000 a person, once airfare and incidentals are figured in.  Sounds like a wonderful trip all the same.  The tour is from September 12 to 22, 2011.
