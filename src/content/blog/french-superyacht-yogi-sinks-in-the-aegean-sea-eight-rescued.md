![](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/yogi.jpg)Looking slightly like a miniature *[Costa Concordia](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2012/01/14/thousands-evacuated-as-cruise-ship-costa-concordia-strikes-reef-off-italy-at-least-three-dead/)*, the  60.2 meter (197′) French yacht *[Yogi](http://www.shipspotting.com/gallery/photo.php?lid=1460798)* rolled and sank after reporting a ‘mechanical failure’ in high seas and Force 8 winds on Friday.  The yacht sank between the islands of Skyros and Psara, 19 nautical miles off the coast.  Greek Coast Guard vessels supported by two Air Force helicopters, a Navy frigate, and four commercial ships headed to the assist the yacht after its captain sent out a distress signal.  [Eight people aboard the *Yogi* were airlifted to safety](http://www.cbsnews.com/8301-202_162-57380313/8-rescued-from-sinking-200-foot-yacht-in-greece/) by Greek Super Puma helicopters.  The yacht *Yogi* was less than a year old, having been built at the Proteksan Turquoise Shipyard in Istanbul, Turkey in 2011.
