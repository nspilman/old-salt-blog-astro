British windsurfer, Steve Thorpe, was recently clocked at 50.48 knots, (58 mph or 93.3 km/hr,) sailing his windsurfer in 40 to 80 knots winds, in West Kirby, England.  If the GPS readings are confirmed, he may hold the title of the world’s fastest windsurfer.

[Steve Thorp hits 50.48 knots in West Kirby](http://www.surfertoday.com/windsurfing/6708-steve-thorp-hits-5048-knots-in-west-kirby)  
  
Over distances of 500 yards, the current record holder for speed under sail is American Robert Douglas on a kite surfer with a speed of 55.65 knots.  For a distance of one nautical mile, the hydrofoil trimaran *[l’Hydroptère](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2010/03/20/hydroptere-%E2%80%93-fastest-on-the-water-sets-sights-offshore/)* holds the record at 50.17 knots.
