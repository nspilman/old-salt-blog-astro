![](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/05/pioneerwavertree.jpg)On Saturday May 24th, the  Southstreet Seaport Museum in New York will celebrate the 125th birthdays of two fine ladies,  the full rigged ship *[Wavertree](http://www.southstreetseaportmuseum.org/index1.aspx?BD=8993)* and the schooner *[Pioneer](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pioneer_\(schooner\))*[.](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pioneer_\(schooner\))

Built in 1885 in Southampton, England, the *Wavertree* was one of the last large sailing ships built of wrought iron.   Also in 1885 the cargo sloop *Pioneer,* built in Marcus Hook, Pennsylvania, was the first of only two American cargo sloops ever built with a wrought iron hull.  Ten years later she was rerigged as a schooner.  The Pioneer regulatory sails from the South Street Seaport.
