![](https://old-salt-blog-media.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/uploads/2018/05/ajmeerwald.jpg)We recently posted about [Portside NewYork’s tanker *Mary A. Whalen*](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2018/05/mary-a-whalen-80th-birthday-art-inspiration-day-birthday-tanker-tours/)‘s 80th birthday. We also posted about the ex-USCG lighthouse tender [*Lilac*‘s 90th birthday, which is tomorrow. (Stop by for cake.)](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2018/05/ex-uscg-lighthouse-tender-lilac-turns-85-with-free-birthday-cake/) And coming up next weekend, June 2, is the oyster sloop, [*A.J. Meerwald*](https://bayshorecenter.org/a-j-meerwald/)‘s 90th birthday. Hosted by [Discover Delaware Bay](https://www.facebook.com/DiscoverDelawareBay/), there will be a [celebration](https://www.facebook.com/events/189003895084070/) with birthday cake, food, music, and birthday sails beginning at 11 a.m. June 2, at the [Bayshore Center](https://bayshorecenter.org/) at Bivalve, in Port Norris, NJ. Sails on the schooner will be available at 12 PM, 3:45 PM, 5:30 PM, and 7:30 PM. The celebration will continue with a Boat Parade at dusk followed by fireworks. 

The Bayshore Center is also celebrating the 20th anniversary of the *Meerwald* being officially designated the Tall Ship of New Jersey.

The Bayshore Center at Bivalve is a non-profit organization that promotes the history, culture, and environment of New Jersey’s Bayshore region through education, preservation and example. It operates the Meerwald as a hands-on sailing classroom. It also operates the Delaware Bay Museum & Folk Life Center and offers shore-based programs at its campus and schooner’s home port of Bivalve in Commercial Township, on the Maurice River in Cumberland County.

[For tickets and more information, click here.](https://bayshorecenter.org/events/)
