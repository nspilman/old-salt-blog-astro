[![WEB-shipsbell06nw2](https://old-salt-blog-media.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/uploads/2014/11/WEB-shipsbell06nw2.jpg)](https://old-salt-blog-media.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/uploads/2014/11/WEB-shipsbell06nw2.jpg)In [September, the wreck](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2014/09/ship-from-franklin-expedition-possibly-the-battle-of-baltimore-found/) of one of the two ships in [Franklin’s lost  expedition](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franklin's_lost_expedition) of 1845 was located near the the Victoria Strait in Canada. As both ships in the expedition were similar sized bomb vessels, modified for exploration, it was initially unclear whether the wreck was from the [*HMS Terror*](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HMS_Terror_\(1813\)) or [*HMS Erebus*](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HMS_Erebus_\(1826\)). By early October, [the wreckage had been confirmed to be from the](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2014/10/confirmed-franklins-hms-erebus-found-in-victoria-strait/) *[HMS Erebus.](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2014/10/confirmed-franklins-hms-erebus-found-in-victoria-strait/)*  Now divers have located the ship’s bell.  [As reported by CNBC](http://www.cnbc.com/id/102160981#.):

*The bell is intact and generally in very good condition. Two embossed markings – introduced when the bronze bell was first cast – are evident on the artifact: a Royal Navy “broad arrow” indicating property of the British Government, as well as the date “1845.”*
