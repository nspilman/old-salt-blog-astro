Kalmar Nyckel

Frankly, the [*A.J. Meerwald*](http://www.ajmeerwald.org/index.htm) , the *[Kalmar Nycke](http://www.kalmarnyckel.org/)l,* and the *[Gazela](http://www.gazela.org/ships/gazela/gazela.htm)* deserve far better.   The *[A.J. Meerwald](http://www.ajmeerwald.org),*  the “Official Tall Ship of the state of New Jersey,” is  a 1928 built Delaware Bay oyster schooner.  The *[Kalmar Nyckel](http://www.kalmarnyckel.org/) ,* the Delaware’s “Official Tall Ship,” is a replica of a 1625 built [pinnance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinnace), which was the flagship of the colony of “New Sweden,”  in much of what is now Delaware.  The 1901 built barkentine *[Gazela Primeiro](http://www.gazela.org/ships/gazela/gazela.htm)* is a Portuguese fishing schooner now lovingly restored in Philadelphia.

These fine vessels will  be play-acting as “pirate” ships this Sunday in mock battles  in an event sponsored by the [Independence Seaport Museum](http://www.phillyseaport.org/) in Philadelphia.  Pairing an oyster schooner, a 15th century replica and a Portuguese barkentine is fine, but letting them be overrun by Disney-fied pirates, well that is a bit much.
