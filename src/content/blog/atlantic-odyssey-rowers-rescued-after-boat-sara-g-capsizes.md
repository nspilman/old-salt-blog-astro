![](https://old-salt-blog-media.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/uploads/2012/02/antlantichallengerowers.jpg)These are busy days in ocean rowing. As the [Talisker Whisky Atlantic Challenge](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2012/01/30/ocean-rowing-round-up-part-1-talisker-whisky-atlantic-challenge-setting-records/) is finishing up and the [Bouvet Guyane Solo Atlantic Rowing Race 2012](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2012/01/30/ocean-rowing-round-up-part-2-bouvet-guyane-solo-atlantic-rowing-race-2012/) is getting started, an attempt at breaking an ocean rowing record as failed, ending with the rescue of six rowers from a life raft raft after their boat, the *Sara G*, capsized and sank. The six men, five from Britain and one from Ireland, were rowing from Morocco to Barbados in the “[Atlantic Odyssey challenge](http://www.atlantic-odyssey.com/),” an attempt to row across the Atlantic in thirty days or less.  After 27 days and still 520 miles from their destination, their boat was capsized by large waves. The a Panamanian container ship, the *[Nord Taipei](http://www.shipspotting.com/gallery/photo.php?lid=1456841)*, was diverted to pick up the rowers after their EPIRB signal was picked up by Falmouth Coastguard.   Thanks to [Alaric Bond](http://www.alaricbond.com/) for passing along the news.
