![](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/USRCS-logo.jpg)On August 4, 1790, Alexander Hamilton founded the [Revenue Cutter Service](Founded by Alexander Hamilton as the Revenue Cutter Service on 4 August 1790, it lays claim to being the United States' oldest continuous seagoing service.), the predecessor of the [United States Coast Guard.](http://www.uscg.mil/)   The anniversary is celebrated in various ways around the United States. [Portsmouth, VA](http://hamptonroads.com/2011/08/happy-221st-coast-guard-let%E2%80%99s-party) will host a celebration today through Saturday. In Alaska, August has been proclaimed “[United States Coast Guard Appreciation Month.”](http://juneauempire.com/local/2011-08-03/coast-guard-celebrates-221st-birthday-official-month-designation)   The [National World War II Museum](http://www.military.com/news/article/coast-guard-news/national-world-war-ii-museum-celebrates-cg-day.html) will also be celebrating the anniversary with a lecture by author Thomas P. Ostrom.
