Kathleen and May

The [Bristol Harbour Festival](http://www.bristolharbourfestival.co.uk/index.php) begins today and runs through the weekend. It is expected to attract over 200,000 people and is one of the biggest free events in the UK.    In addition to [Brunel’s](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2010/07/06/ss-great-britain-from-seabed-to-national-treasure-a-model-for-ship-restoration/) *[SS Great Britain](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2010/07/06/ss-great-britain-from-seabed-to-national-treasure-a-model-for-ship-restoration/)*, on permanent display in Bristol, the harbor will be visited by the three-masted schooner *[Kathleen and May](http://www.kathleenandmay.co.uk/index.html)*, as well as the  *[Matthew](http://www.matthew.co.uk/)*, a replica of explorer John Cabot’s ship from 1497. Also visiting for the festival is the *[Vilma](http://www.jotpix.com/vyt.aspx?yy=WgGl_pm585k)* an ex Danish fishing boat from 1934, rerigged like an old Irish trading schooner.
