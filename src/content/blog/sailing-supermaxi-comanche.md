Last November [we posted about the initials tria](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2014/11/supermaxi-comanche-on-sea-trials/)[ls](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2014/11/supermaxi-comanche-on-sea-trials/) of Jim Clark and Kristy Hinze-Clark’s new 100’ carbon fiber speed-demon *[Comanche](http://www.sail-world.com/USA/New-supermaxi-Comanche-sails-with-Ken-Read-ahead-of-Hobart-race/127744).*  Intended to be the fastest monohull in the world, it lived up to that claim during this summer’s [Transatlantic Race](http://www.transatlanticrace.org/2015-race/687-transatlantic-race-2015-now-in-the-history-books) where *Comanche* set a new record by sailing 618.01 miles in a 24-hour period, averaging 25.75 knots, becoming the first monohull to break the 600-mile barrier.
