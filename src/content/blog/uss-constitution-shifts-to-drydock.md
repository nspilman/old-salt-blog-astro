Last night, the [USS *Constitution* was shifted to Dry Dock #1](http://www.navy.mil/submit/display.asp?story_id=87175) at the Charlestown Navy Yard in Boston. The ship, the world’s oldest commissioned warship afloat, will be undergoing a three year refurbishment. [USS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USS_Constitution) *[Constitution](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USS_Constitution),* nicknamed “*Old Ironsides*,” entered service in the U.S. Navy on Oct. 21, 1797 and remains a commissioned U.S. Navy warship.  Since 1907, the ship has been on display opening her decks to the public. An interview with Naval History and Heritage Command Director Sam Cox.
