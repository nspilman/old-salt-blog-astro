![](https://old-salt-blog-media.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/uploads/2011/06/18.jpg)The [US Postal Service is issuing a set of four stamps honoring the US Merchant Marine](http://www.usps.com/communications/newsroom/2010/pr10_125.htm#18th) throughout its history.  The four stamps feature a clipper ship modeled after the *[Sovereign of the Seas](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_of_the_Seas_\(clipper\))*, launched in 1852; an auxiliary steamship, based on the ships of the [Collins Line](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Collins_Line); a World War II [Liberty Ship](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberty_ship); and a container ship, based on [Matson Line’](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matson_Navigation_Company)s *R.J. Pfeiffer*.   Illustrator [Dennis Lyall](http://www.beyondtheperf.com/bios/stamp-artists/dennis-lyall) of Norwalk, Connecticut, created the stamps under the art direction of Phil Jordan of Falls Church, Virginia.   These are not the first stamps honoring the U.S. merchant marine.   See also [Merchant Marine Stamp of 1946, Steamship Stamp of 1944, and First Day Covers](http://www.usmm.org/fdc.html).

Thanks to Irwin Bryan for passing the news along.
