![](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/archny.jpg)In 1614, Dutch sailor [Adriaen Block](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adriaen_Block) explored the over 70 islands of the archipelago between the mouth of the Hudson and Fisher’s Island. Four hundred years later, photographer, videographer, and writer [Thomas Halaczinsky](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Halaczinsky), set off on his 30-foot sailboat, on a multi-year journey, covering 3,000-miles, to trace Block’s course across the watery world in the shadows of a mighty city and nearby seaways. The photographic and literary logbook of his journey has recently been published as [*Archipelago New York*](https://www.amazon.com/Archipelago-New-York-Thomas-Halaczinsky/dp/0764355074).  

On June 18th, at 6:00pm, there will be a party and book talk to celebrate the publication at [Rizzoli Bookstore](https://www.rizzolibookstore.com/news/archipelago-new-york-thomas-halaczinsky) at 1133 Broadway, between 25th and 26th Street, in Manhattan. The illustrated book talk begins at 6:15 pm.

Photographs from the book, “[Archipelago New York – A secret island world in plain sight](http://www.waterfrontmuseum.org/events/exhibit/archipelago-new-york-%E2%80%93-secret-island-world-plain-sight-thomas-halaczinsky-june-28-%E2%80%93),” will also be on display at the [Waterfront Barge Museum](http://www.waterfrontmuseum.org/museum) from June 28 – July 21 with an Artist’s Reception on June 28th from 7-9pm. The Waterfront Museum is alongside at 290 Conover St, Brooklyn, NY. To learn more check out the [*Archipelago New York* website](http://archipelago-newyork.com/)*.*
