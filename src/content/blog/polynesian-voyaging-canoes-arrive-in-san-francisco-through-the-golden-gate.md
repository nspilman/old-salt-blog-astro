In June, [we posted about Polynesian voyaging canoes of the “Te Mana o Te Moana” (Spirit of the Sea) expedition](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2011/06/18/polynesian-voyaging-canoes-arrive-in-hilo/) arriving in Hilo, Hawaii after sailing from New Zealand by way of the Marquesas and Tahiti.    Yesterday, [six of the vaka, traditional voyaging canoes, from several island nations sailed into San Francisco through the Golden Gate](http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/blogs/hawaii/detail?entry_id=94575&tsp=1). [Plans are shaping up for a a celebration of greeting](http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/blogs/hawaii/detail?entry_id=93981) to be held on Treasure Island this weekend.   Thanks to [Bonnie](http://frogma.blogspot.com/) for pointing out their arrival.
