![1398890327000-lobsters](https://old-salt-blog-media.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/uploads/2014/05/1398890327000-lobsters.jpg)Last August we asked “[Will The Maine Lobster Boom End in a Bust?](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2013/08/the-ecology-and-economics-of-lobster-will-the-maine-lobster-boom-end-in-a-bust/)” It doesn’t necessarily look like a bust, but scientists say that they now are seeing a drop in the number of baby lobsters on the bottom of the Gulf of Maine. After catching an average of 20 million pounds per year for decades, over the last several years, Maine’s lobster-men landed a record 125 million pounds of lobster. With the decreased number of baby lobsters being observed, many worry that they may be seeing the end of the lobster boom.
