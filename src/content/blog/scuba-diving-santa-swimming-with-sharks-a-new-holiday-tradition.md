Scuba Santa swimming with sharks may be a relatively new Christmas tradition but it is remarkably widespread. From the [Newport Aquarium](http://www.cbsnews.com/stories/2005/12/12/earlyshow/main1117728.shtml) in Newport, Kentucky; to the [North Carolina Aquarium](http://www.wcti12.com/news/29959814/detail.html) at Pine Knoll Shores and the [South Carolina Aquarium](http://youtu.be/NO6hqV9rLeE) in Charleston;  to the [Point Defiance Zoo & Aquarium](http://www.q13fox.com/news/kcpq-santa-swims-with-sharks-20111125,0,3019319.story) in Tacoma, WA.  There are also Scuba Santas at the [Holland Aquatic Center](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h2scuk3j-9s), in Holland, Michigan; and the [Tennessee Aquarium](http://youtu.be/tmgHrAoGllk), in Chattanooga, TN. Then there is the [Adventure Aquarium](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bgA0-AkW3sM) in Camden, NJ;  [Oklahoma Aquarium,](http://www.tulsaworld.com/scene/article.aspx?subjectid=668&articleid=20111224_11_A2_CUTLIN431490)  in Tulsa Oklahoma; the [Virginia Aquarium](http://youtu.be/PKaQrwL0xuk), in Virginia Beach, Virgina; and the [South Texas Aquarium](http://youtu.be/wDbmtjyxCKs), Corpus Christi – all with Scuba Santas.  Not all Scuba Santas swim in aquarium tanks. Here are Scuba Santas diving [California’s Channel Islands](http://youtu.be/aCzfuIanGgw) and in [Florida’s Keys](http://youtu.be/ThzKHMmsE9M).  Scuba Santas also make appearances [Sports Chalet dive shops](http://www.sportchalet.com/category/promos/2011/scuba+santa+underwater.do) at locations throughout California, Arizona, Nevada and Utah.

[Scuba Santa](http://youtu.be/NO6hqV9rLeE)  
  

The phenomenon is not limited to the United State. The Scuba Santa at the [Shanghai Ocean Aquarium in Pudong](http://www.treehugger.com/natural-sciences/santa-swims-with-sharks-to-save-the-species-video.html), China sends an environemtnal message about saving endangered sharks.  The [Oceanarium in St. Petersburg, Russia](http://youtu.be/pyoM2EPlfSw) also features a Scuba Santa as does the [Underwater World](http://youtu.be/fpaphdkqhs4) in Mooloolaba, on the Sunshine Coast of Queensland, Australia.

I wonder how you say, “Ho, Ho, Ho” underwater.
