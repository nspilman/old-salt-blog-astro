[Delayed for months by both hurricanes and the pandemic](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2020/07/update-facing-hurricanes-removal-of-car-carrier-golden-ray-delayed-by-coronavirus/), Versabar’s heavy-lift catamaran [VB-10,000, nicknamed the “*Golden Arches,”*](http://www.vbar.com/vb10000/index.html) arrived yesterday at St. Simons Island, GA to begin the scrapping of the wrecked car carrier, [*Golden Ray.*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MV_Golden_Ray) The car carrier rolled on its side in September of 2019 as it departed the Port of Brunswick, GA, carrying about 4,200 vehicles. [VB-10,000](http://www.vbar.com/vb10000/index.html) is the largest heavy-lift vessel ever built in the United States. It will use chains to slice the *Golden Ray* into eight sections, to be placed on barges, and towed away to be scrapped.
