![](https://old-salt-blog-media.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/uploads/2019/08/lilacbowsprite.jpg)If you are in New York City this evening, head over to Pier 25 on the Hudson River to the historic lighthouse tender the ex-[USCGC *Lilac*](http://www.lilacpreservationproject.org/) for a free concert in honor of [National Lighthouse Day](https://nationaldaycalendar.com/national-lighthouse-day-august-7/).  The concert, from 6:00 to 9:00 PM, rain or shine, will be on-deck under the awning, featuring musical performances by [Peter Zummo](https://www.npr.org/2018/03/09/592039983/songs-we-love-peter-zummo-song-ii-left-on-the-beat-variations), [Ernie Brooks](https://lilacpreservationproject.us2.list-manage.com/track/click?u=2a197f1ae59cfefb2ce14c8be&id=a198171c09&e=ffd12cef29), and [Mustafa Khaliq Ahmed.](https://lilacpreservationproject.us2.list-manage.com/track/click?u=2a197f1ae59cfefb2ce14c8be&id=8b7be89603&e=ffd12cef29)

There will be a cash bar serving beer and wine with proceeds benefitting the restoration of the *Lilac* and ship programming. 21+ only.

The USCGC *Lilac* (WAGL/WLM-227) is a former Coast Guard lighthouse tender located in New York City. The Lilac is America’s only surviving steam-powered lighthouse tender. She was added to the National Register of Historic Places on January 7, 2005. She is a museum ship, docked at Pier 25, near North Moore Street in Manhattan.
