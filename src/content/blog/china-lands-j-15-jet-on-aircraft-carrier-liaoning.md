At the end of last September, the [New York Times](http://www.nytimes.com/2012/09/26/world/asia/china-shows-off-an-aircraft-carrier-but-experts-are-skeptical.html?_r=0) quoted un-named “Chinese and other military experts”  regarding the [*Liaoning*](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_aircraft_carrier_Liaoning), the first Chinese aircraft carrier to go into service, that “*China does not have planes capable of landing on the carrier and so far training for such landings has been carried out on land.”*  That is no longer the case, as the Chinese released video over the weekend of a [J-15,](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shenyang_J-15)  *[Flying Shark](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shenyang_J-15),* fighter jet landing and taking off on the carrier.  Thanks to [Alaric Bond](http://alaricbond.com/) for contributing to the post.
