As we [posted](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2012/04/16/opsail-2012-getting-underway-in-new-orleans-then-sailing-for-new-york/) a few days ago, [OpSail 2012](http://www.opsail.org/extra.php) kicks off today in [New Orleans](http://nolanavyweek.com/schedule-of-events-april-17-23-2012.html).  In addition to the [USCG Cutter *Eagle*](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USCGC_Eagle_\(WIX-327\)), two lesser known tall ships will be participating —  Indonesian Navy’s steel three masted barquentine *[Dewaruci](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KRI_Dewaruci),* and the Ecuadoran Navy’s steel three-masted barque *[Guayas](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BAE_Guayas_\(BE-21\))*, commissioned in 1997.  The video below is of the *Dewaruci*‘s port call in San Deigo in March. After the jump is a video of the *Guayas* arriving in New Orleans.
