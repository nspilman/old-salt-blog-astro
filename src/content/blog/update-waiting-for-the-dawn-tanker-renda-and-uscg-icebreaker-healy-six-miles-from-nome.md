![](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/rendabullseye.jpg)The Russian tanker *[Renda](http://www.vesseltracker.com/en/Ships/Renda-8129618.html)*, carrying 1.3 million gallons of emergency fuel, escorted by the USCG icebreaker *[Healy](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USCGC_Healy_\(WAGB-20\))* are within 6 NM of Nome, Alaska  on Friday morning.  They are waiting for sunrise before advancing closer. Daylight will make moving though the ice safer and will help to pinpoint the staging area from whence they can start discharging the cargo of diesel oil and gasoline.  Dawn in Nome today arrives at Nome at around 11:30 AM local time  (9:30 PM GMT.)
