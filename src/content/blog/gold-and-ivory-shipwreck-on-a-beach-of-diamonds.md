![diamond-wreck-615](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/09/diamond-wreck-615.jpg)The October 2009 [National Geographic magazine](http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/2009/10/shipwreck/smith-text/2) has a fascinating story of a 16th century shipwreck on a beach in southern Namibia.  In 1533 a  Portuguese trading ship carrying a fortune in gold and ivory, bound for the famed spice ports of India, was swept ashore and wrecked by a winter storm.  It lay undisturbed until it was discovered only last year.   Ironically,  the treasure laden ship, believed to be the nau *Bom Jesus*, wrecked on a beach rich with diamonds.  The wreck was discovered in the [Sperrgebiet](http://books.google.com/books?id=Ky9G1y_johQC&pg=PA229&lpg=PA229&dq=Sperrgebiet,+the+Forbidden+Zone&source=bl&ots=MRGi9DqhgN&sig=gxxar7em4-yv4RXOdvxhOYRHVGs&hl=en&ei=zoezSri8AoitlAeD84j5Dg&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=5#v=onepage&q=Sperrgebiet%2C%20the%20Forbidden%20Zone&f=falsehttp://books.google.com/books?id=Ky9G1y_johQC&pg=PA229&lpg=PA229&dq=Sperrgebiet,+the+Forbidden+Zone&source=bl&ots=MRGi9DqhgN&sig=gxxar7em4-yv4RXOdvxhOYRHVGs&hl=en&ei=zoezSri8AoitlAeD84j5Dg&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=5#v=onepage&q=Sperrgebiet%2C%20the%20Forbidden%20Zone&f=false), the Forbidden Zone, the fabulously rich and strictlyoff -limits De Beers diamond-mining lease near the mouth of the Orange River on Namibia’s southern coast.
