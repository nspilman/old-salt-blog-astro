![](https://old-salt-blog-media.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/uploads/2011/10/hauntedshiplogoth.jpg)Who needs a haunted house if you can have a haunted ship? Around this time of year, historic and modern ships suddenly become haunted and ghostly. (To be fair, some are reported to be haunted all year round.) Here is a round-up of a lucky thirteen haunted or ghostly ships open to the public around Halloween. Check the links for dates, times and locations.  
  
[Nauticus Museum](http://www.nauticus.org/) in downtown Norfolk, VA is giving visitors [haunted tours of sections of the USS *Wisconsin*](http://www.nauticus.org/programs-activities/bb64-paranormal-investigation).

[The Lake Superior Marine Museum Association](http://www.lsmma.com/) is hosting the [*William A. Irvin* Haunted Ship Tours](http://duluthhauntedship.com/) in Duluth, MN.

In Erie, PA the Flagship *[Niagara](http://flagshipniagara.org/flagship_niagara/index.htm)* is hosting [Ghosts Afloat](http://www.ghostsafloat.org/).

The [Battleship *North Carolina*](http://battleshipnc.com/) is transformed into a [Ghost Ship](http://battleshipnc.com/Events/GhostShip.aspx) in Wilmington, NC.

[Blackbeard’s ghost](http://www.patriotspoint.org/news_events/blackbeards-ghost-on-the-yorktown-28-31-october/) is expected to make an appearance on the [USS *Yorktown* at Patriots Point](http://www.patriotspoint.org/), Mount Pleasant, SC.

In Washington D.C. the [Ghost Ship *Barry*](http://www.navyhistory.org/event/66/ghost-ship-barry/) is accepting visitors on Friday night.

The [U.S. Coast Guard Cutter *Mobile Bay*](http://www.uscg.mil/d9/cgcmobilebay/) in Sturgeon Bay, WI is being turned into a [Ghost Ship](http://www.greenbaypressgazette.com/article/20111019/ADV05/110190654/Coast-Guard-cutter-becomes-Ghost-Ship).

In Newport Beach, CA, the [Ghost Ship](http://ghostship.com/) sets sail.

In Columbus, Ohio, the [*Santa Maria* becomes a Haunted Ship](http://www.santamaria.org/index.php).

On the [USS *Hornet*, Alameda CA’s Haunted Aircraft Carrier](http://www.hauntedbay.com/features/usshornet.shtml), Halloween is being celebrated with a [Monster Bash](http://www.uss-hornet.org/calendar/halloween/)!

In Quincy, MA the [USS *Salem* becomes the Haunted Ship](http://www.hauntedship.com/)

The windjammer *[Star of India](http://www.sdmaritime.org/star-of-india/)* in San Diego, CA, which is reported to be haunted by at least two sailors year round, is hosting a [Halloween Carnival](http://www.nbcsandiego.com/the-scene/events/Halloween-on-the-Haunted-Ship-67200652.html).

In Wildwood, NJ, [Morey’s Piers](http://www.moreyspiers.com/fears/) at Mariner’s Landing is featuring “Terror on the Boardwalk” with their own [Ghost Ship.](http://www.courierpostonline.com/article/20111013/ENT/310130047/Morey-s-transforms-Mariner-s-Landing-Halloween) 

In Newport, Kentucky, on the Ohio River, [USS Nightmare, is America’s Premier Haunted Steamboat](http://www.ussnightmare.com/history-design.html).  (Thanks to Tim Koch for pointing it out.)
