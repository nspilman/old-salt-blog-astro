![](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/usspueblo.jpg)Fifty years ago today, on January 23, 1968, [North Korea seized the U.S. Navy intelligence ship](https://www.nytimes.com/aponline/2018/01/23/us/ap-history.html) [USS *Pueblo*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USS_Pueblo_\(AGER-2\)) while operating in international waters. One sailor was killed and 82 were taken prisoner. The *Pueblo* was commanded by Lloyd “Pete” Bucher. He and his crew were charged with spying on North Korea. Commander Bucher and his crew were released the following December after enduring 11 months of brutal captivity at the hands of the North Koreans. *Pueblo* is still listed as a commissioned U.S. Navy vessel — the only one held by a foreign nation. The ship is a now a tourist attraction in Pyongyang, North Korea.
