With decreased pollution, the [Chesapeake Bay has seen a significant recover](http://news.maryland.gov/dnr/2017/04/19/survey-finds-resilient-chesapeake-bay-blue-crab-population/)y in its blue crab population in recent years. All is not good news, however. The 2017 [crabbing season was cut short](https://www.chesapeakebaymagazine.com/baybulletin/2017/6/27/new-crabbing-limits-as-population-declines) by a significant population decline. With luck, the drop will only be short term.
