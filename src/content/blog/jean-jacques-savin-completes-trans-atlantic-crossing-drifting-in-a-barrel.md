![](https://old-salt-blog-media.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/uploads/2019/04/savinbarrel.jpg)After a voyage of 147 days covering more than 2,000 nautical miles, French adventurer [Jean-Jacques Savin](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2018/12/french-adventurer-drifting-across-the-atlantic-in-a-barrel/), 72, has successfully drifted across the Atlantic Ocean on the wind and currents in his 10′ by 9′ orange barrel-shaped  craft.

He departed from El Hierro, one of the Canary Islands, west of Morocco, at the end of last December.  After [arriving in the Caribbean on May 2](https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/2019/05/08/french-adventurer-completes-trip-across-atlantic-ocean-huge-barrel/?utm_term=.5c80e86ab216), an oil tanker towed Savin and his barrel to the tiny Dutch island of St. Eustatius. His [Facebook page reports](https://www.facebook.com/BOUTESA/) that he is now on board a cargo ship on its way to Martinique.
