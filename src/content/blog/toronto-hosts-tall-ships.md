![](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/torontoships.jpg)The [Redpath Toronto Waterfront Festival](http://www.towaterfrontfest.com/) started yesterday and will run through July 4th.   The festival is hosting [fifteen sailing ships and schooners](http://www.towaterfrontfest.com/ships.asp), including *[HMS Bounty](http://www.tallshipbounty.org/)*, the [brig](http://flagshipniagara.org/flagship_niagara/index.htm) *[Niagara](http://flagshipniagara.org/flagship_niagara/index.htm)*, the [Bark](http://www.barkeuropa.com/en/home/1-zeilschip_bark_EUROPA) *[Europa](http://www.barkeuropa.com/en/home/1-zeilschip_bark_EUROPA)*, the *[Pride of Baltimore II](http://www.pride2.org/index.php)*,  [Brig](http://www.sailtraining.de/roaldamundsen.html?&L=1) *[Roald Amundsen](http://www.sailtraining.de/roaldamundsen.html?&L=1), t*he schooner *[Roseway](http://www.worldoceanschool.org/)* and the topsail schooner *[Unicorn](http://www.tallshipunicorn.com/).* The tallships are part of the [Great Lakes United TALL SHIPS CHALLENGE](http://www.sailtraining.org/tallships/2010greatlakes/index.php), a series of tall ship port to port races coordinated by the [American Sail Training Association (ASTA)](http://www.sailtraining.org/) across all five Great Lakes.  Toronto will be the first and only Canadian port.
