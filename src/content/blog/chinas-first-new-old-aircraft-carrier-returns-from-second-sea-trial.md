![](https://old-salt-blog-media.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/uploads/2011/12/u1_Stage-Sea-Trials.jpg)Last Sunday the first Chinese aircraft carrier returned from its second round of sea trials. The as of yet unnamed carrier, which reports suggest will be christened *[Shi Lang](http://http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2011/08/10/from-varyag-to-shi-lang-the-long-strange-voyage-of-chinas-new-twenty-year-old-aircraft-carrier/)*, was originally the unfinished Russian aircraft carrier *Varyag* whose keel was laid in 1985. The carrier is reported to be still missing gear necessary to land planes on the deck, due to a refusal of Russia to sell the equipment to China.  (See our previous post – [From Varyag to Shi Lang – the Long Strange Voyage of China’s New Twenty Year Old Aircraft Carrier](http://www.oldsaltblog.com/2011/08/10/from-varyag-to-shi-lang-the-long-strange-voyage-of-chinas-new-twenty-year-old-aircraft-carrier/).)
